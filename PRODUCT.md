# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three distinct groups:
- **Clients**: South Africans (primarily urban, mobile-first) finding a legitimate nearby
  therapist or spa. Trust and legitimacy are the deciding factors at browse time.
- **Therapists and spas**: Independent practitioners and small businesses self-managing
  their listing, subscription, and photos. Need clarity and control without complexity.
- **Admins**: Small team, desktop, vetting registrations and managing location data.
  Efficiency and clear hierarchy matter more than visual polish.

## Product Purpose

A vetted, three-sided massage therapy directory for South Africa. Clients discover and
contact legitimate therapists and spas. Suppliers register and maintain a subscription.
Admins approve, reject, and investigate listings to keep the directory trustworthy.
Success: a client finds a real, nearby therapist without doubt about legitimacy.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, local.

Calm and direct: no wellness marketing fluff, no corporate cold, no hustle. Speaks
like a knowledgeable friend recommending someone they trust.

## Anti-references

- **Gumtree.co.za**: cluttered classifieds feel, transactional, low-trust, no curation signal
- **Netcare.co.za**: clinical, institutional coldness — the opposite of warmth
- **Adult/escort directory sites**: the most critical anti-reference. Any visual or layout
  pattern that could read as seedy or suggestive is a hard failure. The interface must
  be unambiguously professional.

## Design Principles

1. **Legitimate at a glance.** A client arriving from Google search should feel safe within
   two seconds. Vetted-and-professional must be immediate and unmistakable.
2. **Warm without fluff.** Human and approachable, but no wellness clichés (lotus flowers,
   flowing gradients, generic stock photos). Warmth through clarity and care, not decoration.
3. **Local, not generic.** South African context is a feature. Don't default to global SaaS
   or classifieds patterns that carry no sense of place.
4. **Trust through restraint.** Clutter reads as low-effort; clarity reads as curation.
   Less noise signals more confidence.
5. **Mobile as the real context.** The client is almost always on a phone, outdoors or in
   transit. Every interaction should feel effortless at thumb-reach.
6. **Colour and visual consistency.** Off-white background (#f8f5f0 or similar) with a warm
   teal/green accent. No dark mode on customer-facing screens. One design language across
   all screens — full platform visual consistency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum
- Mobile-first at 375px base width
- Touch targets minimum 44×44px
- Reduced-motion safe; no motion-critical interactions
